Innovative treatment for psychiatric disorders

Author: Cristian Andrés Cárdenas · Source: Nature

Potential applications of temporal interference deep brain stimulation for the treatment of transdiagnostic conditions in psychiatry

In psychiatry, brain stimulation techniques are used to treat problems such as depression, anxiety, obsessive compulsive disorder, or post-traumatic stress disorder. Techniques such as DBS (Deep Brain Stimulation) is effective but requires invasive surgery to implant electrodes. It’s expensive and carries medical risks. And TMS (Transcranial Magnetic Stimulation), which is non-invasive, but only reaches the outer cortex of the brain, not deeper areas like the amygdala or striatum.

This limits both research and treatment options, so a new proposal has been developed: Temporal Interference (TI)

A non-invasive method that applies high-frequency electrical currents from outside the head (via scalp electrodes). The trick is that the brain does not respond directly to these very high frequencies, but when two slightly different frequencies are applied, in the overlap area they create a “low-frequency envelope.” That envelope is within the range of natural brain activity, so it can stimulate deep brain regions without affecting the surface.

Two electric fields (f1, f2) deliver inhibitory stimulation to the amygdala at extra-physiological frequencies, (e.g., f1 = 5000 Hz and f2 = 5160 Hz), where T is the target frequency that represents the difference between the two (e.g., 160 Hz).

Applications in psychiatry:

PTSD (post-traumatic stress disorder): inhibit the amygdala to reduce exaggerated fear responses; Depression with anhedonia (lack of motivation/pleasure): stimulate the ventral striatum to boost motivation; Brain mapping: use TI to explore how deep areas are connected and how they influence behavior.

This article suggests that temporal interference brain stimulation can non-invasively and precisely target deep brain regions, making it a promising future method to treat psychiatric conditions such as depression, anxiety, and PTSD.
