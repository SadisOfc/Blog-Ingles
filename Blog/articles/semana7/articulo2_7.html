Colombia Faces a Birthrate Crisis: Births Drop Over 30% in a Decade

Author: Jose Miguel Garcia · Source: El Colombiano

The numbers are striking: fewer and fewer children are being born in Colombia. According to the latest data from the Dane, in 2024 there were 453,901 births, representing a 12% drop compared to 2023 and a 31.3% decline compared to 2015. And the trend in 2025 shows no signs of recovery.

This means that in just ten years, the country has lost more than 200,000 births. To put this into perspective, today Colombia has nearly one-third fewer babies than a decade ago.

Studies and surveys point to several reasons. The most common include the high cost of living, changes in family structures (fewer marriages, more people living alone), and the decision of many young people to prioritize studies or work over parenthood

But this is not just a local trend. Globally, fertility rates are also in free fall: from 5 children per woman in 1970 to 2.2 today, with projections showing further decline.While some view this as progress in terms of personal autonomy and gender equity, experts warn that an aging population will bring serious challenges. Healthcare systems and pension funds will face increasing pressure, as there will be fewer young workers and more elderly people to support.

In fact, Colombia’s fertility rate reached its lowest point in history in 2024: an average of 1.1 children per woman, well below the replacement level of 2.1.
