China blocks U.S. semiconductors, pushing local firms to boost their own AI chips.

Author: Juan Pablo Dueñas · Source: Xataka

The United States and China are in the midst of a commercial and technological war, and one of the most tense areas is semiconductors. This week, China decided to ban its local companies from purchasing and testing Nvidia chips, a move that highlights the tension between both countries and China’s effort to reduce its dependence on U.S. technology.

The measure directly affects giants such as Alibaba and ByteDance, which until now had used foreign chips to train and deploy their artificial intelligence models. With the ban, orders were canceled and ongoing tests were halted, sending a clear message: the future of AI in China must rely on domestic components and not on imports conditioned by the United States.

Meanwhile, Chinese companies such as Huawei and Cambricon are doubling down on efforts to develop processors capable of competing with those of Nvidia. According to authorities, there are already local alternatives with comparable performance, which allows them to claim technological self sufficiency, at least in the use of processors. The ban, instead of slowing down Chinese tech firms, seems rather to push them to accelerate the adoption and improvement of their own solutions.
