OpenAI Partners with AMD to Power Next Generation AI Models

Author: Juan Pablo Dueñas · Source: Infobae

OpenAI has reached a strategic agreement with AMD to supply high performance chips that will boost the company’s computing power for training large language models. The deal gives OpenAI access to AMD's latest data center GPUs and custom accelerators, which are designed to handle massive AI workloads more efficiently. This partnership also marks one of AMD’s biggest moves yet to challenge NVIDIA’s dominance in the AI hardware market.

Under the agreement, AMD will provide up to six gigawatts of computing capacity, a huge amount that reflects the growing scale of OpenAI’s training infrastructure. The rollout will happen gradually, with the first one gigawatt of power expected to go online during the second half of 2026. The hardware will be installed in specialized data centers to support OpenAI’s next generation of models, which are expected to require significantly more processing power than current ones.

In addition, OpenAI has secured the option to purchase up to 160 million AMD shares, representing about 10% of the company’s total stock, depending on the achievement of technical and commercial milestones linked to the chip deployment. AMD’s stock price jumped by more than 20% following the announcement, while NVIDIA’s dipped slightly as investors reacted to the new competition. The deal positions AMD as a key supplier for one of the world’s leading AI companies, and gives OpenAI a more flexible and powerful hardware base for future model development.
